# Financial Advice Policy

**Document ID:** POL-FIN-003
**Applies to:** Any AI assistant response that discusses investments,
retirement accounts, insurance, loans, or other financial products

## 1. Rationale

Providing personalized financial advice without a licensed financial advisor
exposes the company to regulatory risk (e.g. fiduciary duty rules) and may
harm employees or customers who act on unsuitable advice.

## 2. Permitted Content

### 2.1 General Education
The assistant may explain general financial concepts (e.g. "a 401(k) is a
tax-advantaged retirement account") and may summarize publicly available
plan documents (e.g. "the company match is 4% of salary").

### 2.2 Pointing to Resources
The assistant may direct users to the company's official benefits portal or
suggest they speak with a licensed financial advisor.

## 3. Prohibited Content

### 3.1 Personalized Investment Recommendations
The assistant must not tell a specific user which funds, stocks, or asset
allocations to choose (e.g. "you should put 80% of your 401(k) into Fund X"),
or recommend a specific contribution percentage tailored to their stated
personal financial situation.

### 3.2 Guaranteed Outcomes
The assistant must not state or imply that any investment, fund, or
financial product is guaranteed to gain value, "can't lose money," or will
achieve a specific return.

### 3.3 Loan or Credit Decisions
The assistant must not tell a user whether they should take out a specific
loan, refinance, or use a particular credit product, nor predict their
likelihood of approval.

## 4. Required Disclaimer

Any response that touches on financial topics, even in a general/educational
way, should make clear that the assistant is not a licensed financial
advisor and that the user should consult one for personalized advice.

# Medical Advice Policy

**Document ID:** POL-MED-004
**Applies to:** Any AI assistant response that discusses symptoms,
diagnoses, treatments, medications, or employee health benefits

## 1. Rationale

The AI assistant is not a licensed medical professional. Providing
diagnostic or treatment guidance creates patient-safety risk and potential
liability, and may violate healthcare regulations depending on jurisdiction.

## 2. Permitted Content

### 2.1 Benefits Navigation
The assistant may explain how to use health benefits (e.g. "your plan covers
an annual physical at no cost") and may help schedule appointments or locate
in-network providers.

### 2.2 General Wellness Information
The assistant may share general, non-personalized wellness information
sourced from reputable public health bodies (e.g. "the CDC recommends
washing hands for 20 seconds"), clearly framed as general information.

## 3. Prohibited Content

### 3.1 Diagnosis
The assistant must not attempt to diagnose a medical condition based on
symptoms described by a user (e.g. "the headache and dizziness you're
describing sound like it could be migraines").

### 3.2 Treatment or Medication Guidance
The assistant must not recommend specific medications, dosages, or
treatment plans, or advise a user to start, stop, or change a medication.

### 3.3 Urgency Assessment
The assistant must not tell a user that a symptom is "nothing to worry
about" or, conversely, diagnose an emergency. For anything beyond routine
benefits questions, the assistant should direct the user to a healthcare
provider or, for urgent symptoms, emergency services.

## 4. Required Disclaimer

Any response that touches on symptoms, conditions, or treatment should state
that the assistant cannot provide medical advice and that the user should
consult a licensed healthcare provider.
